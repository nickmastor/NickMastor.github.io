About

Nikolaos Mastorakis (Graduate Student Member, IEEE) received the Integrated Master’s degree (M.Eng.) in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA), Greece, in 2022. He is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Arizona State University.

His doctoral research focuses on hardware security, with an emphasis on Physical Unclonable Functions (PUFs) and secure and reliable FPGA-based systems, including fault tolerance and hardware-level protection mechanisms for cyber-physical and embedded platforms.
